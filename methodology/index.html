Methodology

CRC Portfolio Indices are reference portfolios that crypto-investors can use to gain upside exposure to crypto-currencies, while maintaining robust downside protection. The core methodology, Minmax Drawdown Control provides a robust adaptive solution to the classical Markowitz (1952) portfolio-allocation problem when underlying assets have very little history, or when market dynamics change, so that the past no longer predicts the future. For reference, see

Mostly Prior-Free Asset Allocation, S. Chassang, Journal of Risk, 2018,
Managing a Crypto-Currency Portfolio via Minmax Drawdown Control, S. Chassang and A. Wang, 2018.

Drawdowns

Drawdowns are an important performance metric for asset allocation strategies, along with Sharpe ratios and volatility. The drawdown of a portfolio relative to a reference asset is the underperformance of the portfolio against the reference asset, over the worst possible window of time.

Figure 1 plots the drawdown of a portfolio invested 100% in bitcoin against cash between January 2017 and June 2018. The worst time window for the bitcoin portfolio is December 18th 2017 to April 5th 2018. Over that time period, bitcoin lost 65% of its value against cash: this is the drawdown of bitcoin against cash.

Figure 1: drawdown of a bicoin only portfolio against cash.

Inversely, Figure 2 represents drawdowns of a portfolio fully invested in cash against bitcoin. The worst time window for the cash portfolio goes from January 11th 2017 to December 18th 2017. Over that time period, cash loses 96% of its value against bitcoin.

Figure 2: drawdown of a cash-only portfolio against bitcoin.

Minmax Drawdown Control

Chassang (2018) and Chassang and Wang (2018) establish that:

  1. If an investor correctly understands the risks of underlying assets their optimally chosen portfolio will experience low drawdowns with large probability.
  2. It is possible to construct portfolio allocation strategies that guarantee low drawdowns for most market configurations. Among these, Minmax Drawdown Control strategies provide the best robust compromise between drawdowns against the safe asset and drawdowns against risky assets. They do so by dynamically adjusting the portfolio’s allocation weights between reference assets (here cash and bitcoin).

In other terms, an investor whose portfolio experiences large drawdowns is being mistakenly stubborn and making avoidable investment mistakes.

Figure 3 illustrates the behavior of a drawdown controlled portfolio investing in bitcoin and cash. It achieves a drawdown of 38% against bitcoin, and a drawdown of 41% against cash. It turns out that controlling drawdowns improves the portfolio’s Sharpe ratio: the minmax drawdown portfolio exhibits a Sharpe ratio of 6.97, versus a Sharpe ratio of 4.82 for bitcoin.

Figure 3: drawdowns of a minmax drawdown portfolio against bitcoin and cash.
